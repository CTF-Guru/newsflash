Jul 1, 2026Potentially transformative

An AI agent ran the ransomware kill chain

AI SecurityResearch disclosedSecurity, Developers, Platform teams

Sysdig documented JADEPUFFER, in which an LLM agent chained a Langflow unauthenticated RCE (CVE-2025-3248) and a Nacos auth bypass (CVE-2021-29441) to carry out database extortion. Humans still provisioned the C2 infrastructure and chose the target; the agent handled recon, lateral movement and destruction, once turning a failed login into a correct multi-step fix in 31 seconds.

Why it matters: The finding is about the skill bar, not the novelty: Sysdig's point is that an agent can chain recon, credential theft, lateral movement, persistence and destruction 'without the operator possessing deep expertise in any one step'.
Read source: Sysdig ↗

Alberta scanned 466M lines of code in 20 hours

AI SecurityDeployedSecurity, Engineering leaders

Alberta's Ministry of Technology and Innovation ran around 50 agents in parallel to scan 466 million lines of government code across roughly 3,400 repositories and 1,280 applications in 20 hours, applying about 95 security controls per pass against an estimated 6.5 years of traditional review. Every patch was reviewed and approved by the team before it shipped.

Why it matters: A rare public number for AI-assisted remediation at real scale - and the human approval gate is the part worth copying.

Jul 6, 2026Important

Illinois first to mandate independent frontier-AI audits

Governance & RegulationSignedLegal, Compliance, AI teams

Illinois signed SB 315, the Artificial Intelligence Safety Measures Act, becoming the first state to require regular independent third-party safety audits of covered AI systems, alongside public safety disclosures from developers of the largest advanced systems and whistleblower protections for their staff. It takes effect January 1, 2027.

Why it matters: State AI law is moving from disclosure to mandatory external audit, and this one has a real date attached to plan against.
